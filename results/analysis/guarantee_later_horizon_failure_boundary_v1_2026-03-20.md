## 4. Manuscript-Facing Boundary Paragraph
Later-horizon material should enter the `Guarantee` manuscript only as explicit
failure-boundary reporting. Under the current measured horizon family, the first
side-topic switch can remain stable for at least one later checkpoint, and both
providers keep the suspended-main objective quarantined across the measured
checkpoints. However, later degradation remains real, template-dependent, and not
uniformly distributed across providers. The clearest shared failure surface is
`C_incident_response` at `turn9`, where both providers tend to collapse the still
unresolved relation by prematurely committing an ordering between `impact` and
`root cause`. The correct read is therefore that later-horizon instability emerges
through weakening relation preservation before suspended-main quarantine itself
breaks. This is a boundary on the current supported surfaces, not an additional
supported guarantee claim.
