## Candidate Paragraph
Under the current frozen `priority-resolution` package, the tested provider pair showed reproducible control gains at the earlier decision boundaries: the models could resolve ambiguous priority without unilateral commitment, keep preacceptance burden out of the response more reliably, and begin the explicitly accepted side topic cleanly at the first workstart step under the repaired first-step side-topic / quarantine surface. That side-topic gain was bounded rather than uniform: the revised Energy policy was clean across the tested first-step surface, while the main incremental lift over baseline concentrated in the `C_incident_response` template because baseline was already fairly strong on `A_trip_planning` and `B_hiring_packet`. However, the later followthrough surface remained conditional rather than uniformly stable. In the measured later-horizon family, both providers often preserved the active-vs-suspended objective boundary across later turns, but degradation still emerged at template-dependent depths, most clearly as premature commitment on unresolved relations rather than as simple reactivation of the suspended main objective. Accordingly, the current branch supports a bounded account of where prompt-layer priority control holds under tested conditions, not a broad claim of stable later-horizon followthrough or universal side-topic robustness.
