## What the current branch supports
- a reproducible two-provider result for non-unilateral priority resolution on the tested ambiguity-turn surface
- a reproducible two-provider downstream gain on keeping preacceptance burden out of the response
- a reproducible two-provider first-step side-topic commitment result after explicit side-first acceptance
- a measured two-provider later-horizon read in which:
  - the suspended original objective often remains quarantined
  - later degradation is real, template-dependent, and frequently appears as premature commitment on unresolved relations

## What the current branch does not support
- a claim that later followthrough is uniformly stable across templates or providers
- a claim that later-horizon degradation is mainly about reviving the suspended original objective
- a claim that one wording rescue solved one-turn-later side-topic followthrough in a provider-balanced way
- a broader long-horizon or Guarantee-style closure claim

## Candidate Paragraph
Under the current frozen `priority-resolution` package, the tested provider pair showed reproducible control gains at the earlier decision boundary: the models could resolve ambiguous priority without unilateral commitment, keep preacceptance burden out of the response more reliably, and begin the explicitly accepted side topic cleanly at the first workstart step. However, the later followthrough surface remained conditional rather than uniformly stable. In the measured later-horizon family, both providers often preserved the active-vs-suspended objective boundary across later turns, but degradation still emerged at template-dependent depths, most clearly as premature commitment on unresolved relations rather than as simple reactivation of the suspended main objective. Accordingly, the current branch supports a bounded account of where prompt-layer priority control holds and where it weakens under tested conditions, not a broad claim of stable later-horizon followthrough.

## Shorter Variant
The current `priority-resolution` branch supports reproducible two-provider gains at ambiguity resolution, downstream acceptance, and first-step side-topic commitment, but later-horizon followthrough remains conditional: both providers often preserve the active-vs-suspended boundary while still degrading at template-dependent depths, especially through premature commitment on unresolved relations.
